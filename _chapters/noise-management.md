# Part III, Chapter 3: Noise Management

---

## Theodore Furlbrow, Master Mason of Lordaeron

I know household noise too well: loose rafters, shouting goats, bad cart wheels, and neighbours who believe grease is optional.

Treat noise like a leak. Stand in corners during wind, mark gaps with chalk, then seal them in dry weather. Rattling shutters need weight; settling beams need patience; roof creaks need inspection before winter.

For neighbour noise, speak when calm. Ask for help fixing the source instead of declaring war at dawn. If someone insists their midnight goat choir is "quiet," ask what can be adjusted and when.

Children remain unsolved by all known masonry. Use thicker walls, move play further from bedrooms, and accept that silence later in life sounds almost as loud as the noise.

Keep one simple routine: evening walk-through, hand on shutters, ear to doors, check for new rattles before night turns them into legends. Most household noise is small work delayed too long.

If a noise complaint hearing sounds like bridge-keeper questions, everyone has already lost.

## Morgum Soothumb, Senior Peon

Noise is good. Noise mean work. Work mean things getting done. Things getting done mean Morgum useful. Morgum like being useful. Place with no noise: suspicious. What they doing in there that make no sound? Nothing good, Morgum think.

Different noise mean different things. Hitting sound: someone working, good. Cracking sound: maybe bad, look at that spot, but finish hitting first. Dripping sound: also look, but less urgent. Scraping sound okay unless it stop sudden — if scraping stop sudden, that mean whatever was scraping found something. Morgum always go look when scraping stop. Has not regretted this. Mostly.

But there is other noise. Night noise. Noise in the wall. This different from work noise. Morgum know work noise. This not work noise. This noise from inside where nothing supposed to be. Could be pipe. Could be animal. Could be other things. Morgum not ask what other things. Morgum have good method: knock back. Three knock, slow. Then wait. If something knock back, okay. Now you know it is in there. If nothing knock back, keep waiting. Patience important. Sometimes it knock back late. Once Morgum wait four day and something knock back on day five. Was pipe. But still. Worth waiting. Patience is how Peon outlast problem.

Morgum also find: singing help. Not nice singing. Work singing. The kind that get rhythm going so the lifting easier. Wall noise don't like work singing. Wall noise go quiet when Morgum sing. This is maybe good outcome. Morgum slightly miss the wall noise after. Was company. But quiet is also okay if work get done.

> *Peasant annotation: The knocking method. I want to be on record as saying I have tried this. I have tried this three times. I am not saying it works. I am saying I understand why a man does it.*

> *Acolyte annotation: The practice of knocking thrice upon interior walls before sleeping is, in fact, a simplification of the Ritual of Establishing Quiet Intent. The Peon has intuited something real and is using it to feel better about pipes. I find this almost moving.*

> *Wisp annotation: The wall does not knock back. The listening does.*

## Vorun the Pallid, Acolyte of the Cult

In consecrated work, silence is not absence of sound; it is controlled sound.

For strict rites, build double stone walls with packed earth between, then seal thoroughly. This keeps out footfalls, voices, and the gutter drip that ruins both sleep and summoning.

For chant rites, the room must hold the exact intended volume. If it echoes wrong, outcomes become memorable in unhelpful ways.

Forbidden sounds are simple: knocking from below, breathing with no body, or a low hum at the third hour. If your ordinary home does this, refresh your sigils. If you have no sigils, consider moving before the house memorizes your footsteps.

> *Peasant annotation: The layered stone and packed earth method is technically correct and I have used it for mill walls. The reason I used it and the reason the Acolyte uses it are, I am very grateful to say, entirely different.*

> *Peon annotation: Chanting sound good. Like work singing but longer words and slower. Morgum can chant. Morgum available if pale man need help.*

> *Wisp annotation: Sound sealed inside does not disappear. It circles. It learns the shape of the room. This is perhaps what was intended all along.*
