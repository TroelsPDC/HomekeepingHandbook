# Chapter One: The Peasant's Account

---

They told us to build a tower. Up on the hill, past the broken fence, near where you can hear the fighting if the wind is right. Alright. Fine. Work is work.

I grabbed my hammer. Grabbed my lumber. Grabbed a bit of rope because you never know. The sun was already up, which meant we were late, which meant the foreman would have something to say about it. He always did.

The hill was steep. My boots slipped twice. Good boots, mostly. They've seen better days.

Geralt was with me. Good lad, Geralt. Quiet. Didn't talk much, which was fine because I didn't need talk, I needed someone to hold the other end of the beam. He was good at that.

We started on the foundation. Dig, set, fill, pack. Dig, set, fill, pack. You get into a rhythm after a while. Work, work. That's all it is. Just work.

The battle was close. Closer than I liked. You could hear the clashing and the shouting and once, a sound I didn't want to think about. But you don't stop. If you stop every time something scary happens, nothing ever gets built.

We laid the first floor by noon. I was hungry. Geralt handed me half a roll. Good lad.

By mid-afternoon the walls were going up. The fighting had moved a bit south, which was a relief. Still noisy, but distant-noisy, which is a different thing.

That's when the arrow came.

I didn't see it. I heard it. Then I heard Geralt sit down — not fall, just sit, like his legs decided they were done for the day. He looked at the arrow in his shoulder like he was confused about how it got there.

I got him behind the wall. Packed the wound. Tore a strip off my shirt. "You're going to be fine," I said, because that's what you say. He nodded like he believed me, which was good, because I wasn't sure I did.

I finished the tower alone. Took longer. Didn't do the top bit as neat as I'd have liked.

When they came to get Geralt, the captain looked up at the tower and nodded. "Good work," he said.

Work, work.

That's all it ever is.
