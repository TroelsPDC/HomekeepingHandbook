# Part III, Chapter 5: What To Do If Your Home Becomes Haunted

---

## Edwin Harford, Master Mason of Lordaeron

First: do not panic. Panic has never repaired anything, and it is particularly unhelpful in situations involving the restless dead.

Second: determine the nature of the haunting. A cold presence in the upstairs passage is usually a draft. Check the window casements. A cold presence that stares at you from the corner while you sleep is less easily explained by a failed seal, and you should consult the local clergyman promptly.

If the clergyman is unavailable — or has recently fled — you may attempt the following: leave bread on the threshold of the affected room. This is traditional. Why it works is not my department. It often does.

Document the haunting. Time of occurrence, location, nature of disturbance. A pattern is a problem you can solve. A pattern is *always* a problem you can solve, and this holds as true for apparitions as for a leaking gutter. If the haunting persists for more than one season, consider whether the spirit has any legitimate grievance regarding the property. In my experience, a ghost with a grievance is correct more often than it is wrong.

## Grubnok son of Grubnok, Senior Peon

Haunted? That mean ghost? Grubnok seen ghost. Ghost not so bad. Ghost don't eat food. Ghost don't steal blankets. Ghost just float around and look sad.

Give ghost old corner. Somewhere not important — corner near the firewood, maybe, or back of the grain store where nobody really goes. Ghost happy, peon happy. Everyone leave each other alone. This is good living arrangement.

If ghost get angry: ask what ghost want. Usually ghost want something very specific from when it was alive. Give it. Ghost calm down. Grubnok has a whole shelf now, just for ghost things. Works good.

If ghost too angry for talking: build more walls. Ghost get confused in new walls. Eventually forget what it was angry about. Most things do, given enough walls.

> *Peasant annotation: I will say this: the Peon's approach here is the most practical thing in this chapter. I did not expect that.*

> *Acolyte annotation: "Give ghost old corner" — this is, technically, a form of shrine installation. We commend it without reservation.*

> *Wisp annotation: The ghost knows what the house was before you arrived. The shelf of ghost things is a good beginning.*

## Brother Naxxramas the Pallid, Acolyte of the Cult

A haunted home is not a problem. A haunted home is a home that has begun to fulfil its deeper purpose.

Every structure that has housed the living will, in time, accumulate resonance. This is not a flaw in the architecture. It is the architecture working as intended. The Lich King does not fault a crypt for containing shades. He considers it a crypt doing its job.

If the haunting has become distracting, we suggest the following:

- Identify the focal point of the spirit's attention. This is usually a room, an object, or a former occupant.
- Do not attempt to remove the focal point. You will only relocate the disturbance, and a displaced haunting is considerably less manageable than a settled one.
- Instead, acknowledge the presence. A single candle at the threshold, renewed each dusk, signals that the space is shared.
- Shared spaces are quiet spaces. Ignored spaces are not.

The undead require acknowledgement. This is one of the few things we have in common with the living, and we note it without irony.

## Several Wisps
### *(translated from ambient moonlight)*

The stone remembers every step that has crossed it.

The threshold remembers every hand that has touched it.

What you call a haunting is a home that has accumulated enough memory to speak. You are surprised by this. You were walking through memories every day, and only now — when one remembers loudly enough to startle you — do you notice where you have been living.

We do not have hauntings. We have very old forests. The difference is one of volume.

Be still. Listen. What is the house saying?

If you cannot hear it yet — sit in the oldest part of the room, after midnight, without a candle. Wait. The house will find its voice. It always does. This is not something to fear. It is something that was always going to happen, and it is good that it is happening now, while you are there to hear it.
