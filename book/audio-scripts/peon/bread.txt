Bread is easy. Morgum does not understand why small pink men make complicated face about bread.

You take grain. All grain. Whatever grain. Oat, wheat, barley, rye — Morgum does not separate them, grain does not have feelings about this. Grind it if you have time. If not, leave it. Little crunchy bits in bread are not problem, they are bonus. Texture. You add water until it is like wet dirt, but less dirty. You add salt. Morgum adds salt twice because first time might not take. Then you press it flat with fist and put it on fire until it stops making steam and smells like cooked thing.

Morgum's bread is good bread. It is brown all the way through. It does not go soft or mouldy because there is nothing soft or mouldable left in it. Morgum's bread has lasted three weeks in pack without change. This is good quality. You want bread that waits for you, not bread that falls apart the moment you leave it alone. That is weak bread. Morgum does not respect weak bread.

Some people say bread must rise. Morgum says: rise is for proud bread. Humble bread stays flat and feeds you anyway. Some people say Morgum's bread is too hard. Morgum says: soak it in broth. Problem solved. You were going to eat soup anyway. Now you have soup bread. Everything is better. If tooth breaks on Morgum's bread, that is weak tooth. Tooth should have been stronger. Bread is not apologising.
