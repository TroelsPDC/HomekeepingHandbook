Okay. There is an area. Morgum will be honest about the area.

North extension, behind the old cart storage. This area. Morgum does not go there. Has not gone in two years, maybe three. Before that, went once. Came out. Felt bad for a week — not sick, just bad, like something in the area had looked at Morgum and made a note. Morgum does not like being made note of by things in back rooms. This is a personal preference and also common sense.

So: spikes. Morgum put good spikes around the area, pointing out. Not the sharpest spikes — sharpest spikes are for enemies that move toward you, for things you are in active conflict with. These spikes are different kind. These spikes are statement. They say: Morgum knows this space is different. Morgum acknowledges the difference in the traditional way. The area is seen. The area is contained. Both things are true at same time. Also Morgum leaves things at the edge. Small things. A bit of food. A stone that felt right to leave. You ask why? Hard to say exactly why. But you are living next to something. It is right to acknowledge what you live next to. Even if you do not understand it. Especially if you do not understand it. The things Morgum leaves are gone by morning. Morgum does not ask where they go. Morgum is doing the correct thing and the thing gets done and that is enough information.

Containment means: here, and there, and everyone knows which is which. The area does not get bigger. Morgum checks often. Has tape measure. Checks the same four points on the edge every week. Area stays same size. This is called: things going okay. Morgum says "so far" because being honest is important and because Morgum does not know what changes "so far" into "no longer so far." Watching for this. Spikes are up. Notes are not being made. This is the update.
