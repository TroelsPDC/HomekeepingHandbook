Ancestors are demanding. Morgum say this not as complaint but as fact, like "sky is heavy" or "digging is how you find out what is underneath." Morgum's ancestors number in the many. Many many. Peons live long enough to have many ancestors, if peons lucky enough to die of something that is not collapsing mine.

Morgum has system. Morgum will explain system.

First corner of hut: great-ancestor Grummish, who dug first tunnel under Orgrimmar before Orgrimmar was Orgrimmar. Grummish get best corner, face east. Offering: two meat, stacked, not touching. Meat touching is disrespectful. Morgum not sure why. This is just how it is.

Second corner: Morgum's mother's mother's mother, who is not known by name but who is known by the way knuckles ache in cold weather. This is how ancestors persist: in the body, not just the shelf. Corner face south. Offering: salt, rough grain, in small pile. Salt for the earth she came from. Rough grain because her life was not smooth grain.

Third corner: ancestors who are uncertain. Morgum not always sure which ancestor is which, especially old ones. This corner is for the general ancestors, the ones whose names went somewhere else. Rock arrangement here: seven rocks, arranged so each rock can see the others. This mean "we see you and we know you are there even if we do not know what to call you." This is important. Name is not same as presence.

Wall near door: no offerings. No ancestors near door. Door is for coming in and going out. Ancestors should not be between you and leaving.

This is infrastructure. Morgum maintain shrine same as maintain floor: regularly, before problem happen.
