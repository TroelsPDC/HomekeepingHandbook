Stone roofs are ideal because they accept time instead of pretending to defeat it. They do not rot, and they rarely surprise you in the night.

If you keep thatch, shingle, or tile, remember this: those materials are always dying politely. Inspect on dry days, mark beyond the visible damage, and patch wider than the wound. Half repairs are invitations.

For stone: inspect after storms, clear channels before winter, repoint early cracks immediately. Neglect arrives gradually, then all at once, usually while you are asleep and newly optimistic.

If rain through the ceiling begins to sound like whispering names, repair at once. If it whispers your own name, repair faster.
