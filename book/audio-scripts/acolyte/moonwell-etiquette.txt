I must be transparent: moonwells have been identified, in three separate Scourge campaign briefings that I have personally reviewed, as a Category Two Offensive Hazard. The light they emit is antithetical to several processes I manage at home. The water is actively inimical to the fundamental principle of the Scourge household, which is that things which have ended should remain in productive relationship with the estate rather than being restored. A moonwell, in a very direct sense, is the opposite of a good host. It heals. It renews. It insists on continuation. These are values I cannot endorse from an operational standpoint.

And yet.

The enchantment work is extraordinary. I say this with full awareness of what I am admitting. Whoever bound that much arcane and natural energy into a stable basin — who achieved thermodynamic equilibrium between perpetual luminescence, hydraulic containment, and magical output without visible infrastructure, without a staff to maintain it, without, as far as anyone has been able to determine, *any* external power source — that person, or those people, or that process, understood something about sustained enchantment that most practitioners do not. I have studied the publicly available texts. I have studied several texts that are not publicly available. I understand the theory. I do not understand how they made it work. This is a rare experience for me, and I find it, against my better interests, something close to admiration.

I would not place a moonwell in a Scourge household. The structural incompatibilities are severe and the counter-productive effects on the local work would be significant. But I can appreciate it from a distance, the way one can appreciate a well-argued position one fundamentally opposes. The craftsmanship is real. The dedication is real. I respect, in my profession, anything that was built to last and has.

One further note, for any acolytes reading this who may be considering the matter empirically: do not touch the water. This is not etiquette advice. This is operational guidance. The effects are reversible but the paperwork is not.
