Allow the infestation to mature. Vermin perform a valuable function in the decomposition cycle — they consume the soft materials before the bone-curators arrive, expediting the transition from clutter to monument. An infestation that has been permitted to run its natural course leaves behind a cleaner substrate than one interrupted prematurely.

If the infestation becomes distracting during ritual, relocate the remains to the outer chamber. Rats, like lesser undead, are drawn to warmth and purpose. Deny them both, and they will disperse into the walls, which is where they should have been to begin with.

Candles placed at threshold intervals have shown some deterrent effect. This is presumably because the rats, too, sense what the light is for.
