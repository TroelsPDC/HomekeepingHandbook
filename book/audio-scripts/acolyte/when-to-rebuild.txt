The question of when to rebuild completely is one the Scourge has considered in some depth, as it applies to structures and to other things. The threshold, from our perspective, is when the soul has sufficient value. I will not elaborate on value to whom, as this is the sort of specification that tends to shift the focus of a conversation unhelpfully. Suffice to say that the evaluation is made, the evaluation has criteria, and the criteria are applied with consistency.

For structures: we use the term *structural memory* to describe the accumulated decisions embedded in a building's frame — the choices of angle and joint and material that define what the building fundamentally is. When this memory becomes ambiguous, repair is misleading. You are not repairing the building; you are overlaying new decisions on compromised old ones, and the result is a structure that is uncertain about itself at depth. This uncertainty tends to express itself at inconvenient moments. Complete reconstruction eliminates the ambiguity. The new structure knows what it is because it was made to know. It does not contain contradictions from three different repair campaigns by three different hands with three different understandings of what the structure was supposed to be.

I will acknowledge, as an act of intellectual honesty, that the question "when to rebuild" as posed by this chapter implies that rebuilding is the exceptional case. From where I work, rebuilding is not exceptional. Rebuilding is simply the later stage of a process that began the moment the first stone was laid. Everything built is being unmade at some rate. Our work is to understand the rate, to influence it where that is useful, and to redirect it where it is not. A structure that has reached the threshold of complete reconstruction has simply arrived at a destination that was always there. We do not mourn this. We prepare for it. We work with what the arrival provides.

Practically: if your repair plan has more line items than your original construction, rebuild. If the repairs cannot be sequenced without first removing a different repair, rebuild. If you have looked at the building for several days running and your primary feeling is one of vague obligation rather than genuine engagement, rebuild. The building knows. It is communicating this through your own reluctance. Listen to the building.
