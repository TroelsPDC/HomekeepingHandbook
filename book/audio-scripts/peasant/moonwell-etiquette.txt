I do not have a moonwell. I am Human, and I live in a modest stone-built structure in a region where the local magical infrastructure runs more to cursed graveyards than to luminous water features, and I have made my peace with this. But I have visited a moonwell — once, on a commission that took me further into Night Elf territory than I would, in retrospect, have chosen to go — and I have opinions, which I will now share, because someone must.

The first thing to understand about visiting someone else's moonwell is that it is *their* moonwell, and you are a guest, and you should conduct yourself as you would at any fine water feature that is also apparently a sacred axis of cosmic energy and an object of deep cultural significance to your hosts. This means: do not lean over it. I cannot emphasise this enough. The edge is usually low and often damp, you are probably tired from travelling, and the water does something to your eyes when you look into it — there is a depth there that does not correspond to the visible dimensions of the basin, and your brain will send a signal to your body that says *look closer*, and you must refuse this signal. Step back. Look at the trees instead. The trees are also looking at you, but at least they are not bottomless.

Do not drop anything in. Not a coin, not a tool, not a bit of bread, not your hat if the wind takes it. I understand the instinct — it is a large body of reflective water and the human mind has been throwing things into large bodies of reflective water since before we had language for why. Resist it. Nothing that goes into a moonwell comes back in the same condition it went in, and this applies to physical objects, to ambient thoughts, and almost certainly to small children, so keep a hand on any you've brought with you.

Do not, under any circumstances, drink from it on a dare. I say this because there will be a dare. There is always a dare when you are standing near something you shouldn't touch. The water is very clean — I will grant it that; it is the clearest water I have ever seen, and I have seen a lot of water in the course of structural waterproofing work — but "very clean" and "safe to drink" are not the same category when the item in question is enchanted to a degree that makes most human mages go quiet and look at their shoes. Bring a waterskin. Fill the waterskin before you leave. Drink from the waterskin like a person.
