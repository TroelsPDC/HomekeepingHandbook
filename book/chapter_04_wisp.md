# Chapter Four: The Wisp's Account

---

The hill breathes.

We come.

---

Stone remembers pressure.  
Wood remembers the tree.  
We remember everything —  
even what we were before light.

---

The other builders do not see the roots beneath their feet. They see the hill. We see what the hill is made of — centuries of rain, of fallen leaves, of small creatures who lived and were absorbed. The hill is patient. We understand patience.

We do not carry tools. We are the tool. The forest lends itself to the work, and we are how the forest moves.

---

Far away: the sound of iron meeting iron.  
Birds lift from the treeline.  
They know before it arrives.  
We knew before them.

---

The one called Geralt smells of bread and honest work. The dark one smells of something older, something that used to be alive and chose differently. The small green one smells of soil and effort and, faintly, of grief.

We do not judge what we perceive. We only perceive.

---

The tower rises.  
Each beam placed is a small prayer.  
To what? To nothing.  
To everything.  
To the act itself.

---

Then: the arrow.

It parted the air the way a stone parts water. We felt it before it arrived. We feel most things before they arrive. The knowing does not make the arriving easier.

The human sat down slowly in the grass.  
The grass bent around him.  
The grass did not mind.

---

The dark one knelt.  
The small one pressed cloth to wound.  
We hovered close.

We cannot heal what iron has opened. But we can hold the light steady. We can make the air around a suffering thing feel, for a moment, less indifferent.

We did what we could.

It was enough. Or it was not enough. The forest does not keep such accounts.

---

They carried Geralt down the hill.  
The hill did not notice.  
We noticed for it.

---

We finished the tower.  
The tower does not know what it is for.  
Neither do we, truly.  
But it stands.

---

Evening now.  
The battle fades like smoke.  
Stars appear, one by one,  
the way truths do —  
slowly, and only in darkness.

---

We will return to the trees.  
We came from light.  
We go back to light.  
The tower remains.

Something always remains.
